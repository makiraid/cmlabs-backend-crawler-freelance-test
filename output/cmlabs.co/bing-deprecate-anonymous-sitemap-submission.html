Bing Deprecate Anonymous Sitemap Submission

Previously, Bing allowed users to submit sitemaps without domain authentication or anonymously. However, due to certain reasons, this feature has been removed. Therefore, webmasters need to find alternatives to ensure their websites remain well-indexed.

Key Takeaways

  • Bing has discontinued the anonymous submission of sitemaps.

  • This action was taken due to abuse by spammers on the Bing search engine. 

  • Users can still submit sitemaps through robot.txt and Bing Webmaster Tool. 

  • This cessation will not affect the ranking, except for websites engaged in spam practices. 

  • Bing is committed to maintaining the user experience with this action.


Anonymous sitemap submission is a method used by users to submit sitemaps to the Bing search engine without requiring authentication or user identification. With this method, users can send their sitemaps through an HTTP request without providing personal information or authenticating their accounts.

However, Microsoft has announced the removal of the anonymous sitemap submission method. This means that users will no longer be able to submit sitemaps to Bing through HTTP requests without authentication. Learn more about the reasons for this removal and its impact on SEO through this article.

 

Reasons for Removing Anonymous Sitemap Submission

Microsoft made an important announcement revealing its decision to discontinue the anonymous submission of sitemaps to the Bing search engine. The motivation behind this step stems from the rampant abuse of this feature by spammers, prompting Microsoft to take action and enhance the search experience for users.

Here are the main reasons for the removal of this Bing search engine feature:

A. Abuse by Spammers

Based on evaluations, Microsoft found that the anonymous submission of sitemaps was frequently abused by spammers. This abuse compromised the integrity of search results, necessitating effective measures to tackle spam.

 

B. Reducing Spam

Apart from the abuse, discontinuing anonymous sitemap submission aims to significantly reduce spam on the search engine results pages (SERPs), providing users with more reliable and relevant search results.

While this change will not directly impact the overall search rankings, it's crucial for website owners currently relying on anonymous sitemap submission to explore available alternatives.

 

Implications for Users

The removal may affect some users who relied on the anonymous sitemap submission feature. However, the majority of Bing users are already utilizing alternative methods, such as robots.txt or Bing Webmaster Tools, to submit their sitemaps.

Therefore, the cessation of anonymous sitemap submissions will not be detrimental to webmasters. For website owners currently relying on this method, they need to seek alternative ways to submit their sitemaps.

 

Submitting Bing Sitemaps through Alternative Methods

Here are two methods website owners can use to submit sitemaps:

Method 1: robots.txt

Website owners can include their sitemap in the robots.txt file, which serves as instructions for search engines, informing them about specific pages on the website that should be indexed. This method involves adding the sitemap URL to the robots.txt file using specific syntax.

 

Method 2: Bing Webmaster Tools

Website owners can take advantage of Bing Webmaster Tools, a free platform offering various site management features, including sitemap submission.

By using this tool, website owners can directly submit their sitemaps to Bing, ensuring their websites are well-indexed.

 

Impact on Search Rankings

As mentioned earlier, the removal of this feature will not directly affect a website's search rankings. However, some benefits are expected on the SERPs, including:

1. Decreased Spam

Discontinuing anonymous sitemap submission may indirectly impact some websites involved in spam practices. By eliminating this submission method, search engines will have signals to evaluate the credibility of potentially spammy websites and lower their rankings.

 

2. Improved Ranking Quality

For websites following recommended white hat SEO practices, the cessation of anonymous sitemap submission will not have a negative impact on their search rankings.

High-quality content, relevant keywords, and authoritative backlinks will continue to be ranking factors. Hence, regular optimization is necessary to maintain website performance on any search engine.

 

Conclusion

Microsoft's decision to discontinue the anonymous submission of sitemaps for Bing demonstrates its commitment to combating spam and enhancing the search experience. Although this change won't directly affect search rankings, website owners relying on anonymous sitemap submission should promptly switch to alternative methods.

By including sitemaps in robots.txt or utilizing Bing Webmaster Tools, website owners can ensure their sitemaps are well-indexed by the Bing search engine.
